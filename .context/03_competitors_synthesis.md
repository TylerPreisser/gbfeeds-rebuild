## 2. Signature moves to ADAPT (not copy)

Each move is named, attributed, and translated for GB Feeds specifically.

### Move 1 — Season-phase as primary navigation taxonomy
**Pioneered by:** Sitka Gear (terrain-as-navigation: Subalpine / Open Country / Elevated II as first-class L1 destinations).
**Adopt:** Replace the current product-type IA (`/products` flat grid) with a season-phase navigation axis: **Pre-Rut / Rut / Post-Rut / Antler-Growth / Year-Round**. Each becomes a destination page with hero photography of that exact phase on Greg's actual Kansas property + the science copy + the SKUs filtered to that phase + a feeder-system prescription.
**Morph for GB Feeds:** This is not abstract terrain-coding — it's *Greg's actual hunting calendar*. Each phase page is annotated with a calendar strip showing the date range, the typical rainfall/temperature in central Kansas, and Greg's stand-by-stand notes from that phase. The taxonomy is editorial, not categorical.

### Move 2 — Spec-as-hero on the Buck Chow PDP
**Pioneered by:** Record Rack (giant 17% / 6% / 22% Crude Protein / Fat / Fiber triptych on every PDP).
**Adopt:** Buck Chow PDP opens with a giant nutrition triptych — **20% PROTEIN / 4% FAT / 8% CALCIUM** — set in the display face at ~200px, replacing what most CPG sites use price for.
**Morph for GB Feeds:** Treat the triptych as a die-stamped feed-bag tag, not a clean spec sheet. Numbers are large, but they sit inside a black-ink rectangle that reproduces the actual feed-bag label vocabulary ("GUARANTEED ANALYSIS", lot-stamp date, manufacturer city — Manhattan, KS). The PDP IS the bag-tag, scaled up to viewport size.

### Move 3 — "Field Club" — named subscription with member-exclusive product
**Pioneered by:** Black Rifle Coffee (1773 Coffee Club + 1773 Roast as member-only flagship).
**Adopt:** Launch **The GB Feeds Field Club** — a named tier where the membership has lore, not just a recurring-shipment toggle. Include one or two member-only blends nobody else can buy.
**Morph for GB Feeds:** The flagship exclusive is a **seasonal Pre-Rut blend** ("Field Club Pre-Rut Mix") shipped only in late-August window timed to the actual Kansas season. Bag art differs from the public Buck Chow line (single-color blackletter date stamp on burlap-feel paper). Pair with "Greg's Letter" — a printed quarterly note in every Field Club shipment, voice-of-the-founder, hand-signed scan.

### Move 4 — "Build your feed program" wizard
**Pioneered by:** Sitka System Builder + BRCC FIND YOUR COFFEE persistent header CTA.
**Adopt:** Persistent top-nav CTA labeled **"BUILD YOUR PROGRAM"** that opens a 3-step wizard: (1) where you hunt (Midwest / Plains / South / Other), (2) season you're targeting (Pre-Rut / Rut / Post-Rut / Year-Round / Antler-Growth), (3) primary goal (Attraction / Antler Growth / Herd Health / All-of-it) → returns a curated 2–3 SKU bundle with a single "Add Bundle to Cart" button.
**Morph for GB Feeds:** The wizard speaks in Greg's voice ("Where you huntin'?"), not corporate copy. The output is rendered as a **handwritten prescription pad** — date stamped, "Rx for [region]: 2 bags Buck Chow + 1 bag Corn Candy" — not a generic "your bundle is ready" card.

### Move 5 — Sticky financing strip on $999+ feeder line
**Pioneered by:** Boss Buck (Klarna + Sezzle on persistent crimson bar).
**Adopt:** A persistent top strip — but only on the four Texas Wildlife Supply feeder PDPs and the `/feeders` category — with **Klarna / Affirm 4 monthly payments of $X**. Below $999 it disappears.
**Morph for GB Feeds:** The strip itself is styled as a **pasted on receipt-tape** — perforated edges, monospace dot-matrix numerals, slight rotational offset. Not a UI bar, a physical artifact. Reinforces the small-batch / hand-built voice.

### Move 6 — `/field-notes` editorial route in Greg's voice
**Pioneered by:** YETI (`/stories` with 75+ films + ambassador profiles) + Whitetail Institute (`Whitetail News` magazine + Field Tester Survey).
**Adopt:** A `/field-notes` route — Greg's stand-by-stand entries, dated, geo-tagged to Kansas counties, photo-led, copy-light. Categories: Pre-Rut Notes, Rut Reports, Antler-Growth Diaries, Customer Hunts.
**Morph for GB Feeds:** Each entry opens with a **hand-stamped page header**: county name, date, weather, wind direction, deer activity score (1–5). Reads like a logbook page Greg keeps in his truck, not a marketing blog. No SEO-padding; one entry every 2–3 weeks tied to actual hunts.

### Move 7 — Hunter-to-hunter testimonial wall as first-class section
**Pioneered by:** GB Feeds itself (already has 22 verbatim short-form testimonials with first names — Aaron, Trevor, Kaden, Dylan…).
**Adopt:** Preserve the stark, no-avatar, first-name-only treatment from the live site. The minimalism *is* the authenticity signal — Big & J and Boss Buck have ZERO reviews; GB has 22 verbatim. That's the moat.
**Morph for GB Feeds:** Render the testimonial wall as a **scrolling marquee ticker** along the bottom of the home page, like a CB-radio transmission log: tiny monospace timestamp + first-name + verbatim quote. Continuous horizontal scroll, never autoplay-paused. ("DUDE this stuff is fire!" — JON, 0341 hours).

### Move 8 — Persistent claim strip with single defensible heritage line
**Pioneered by:** Big & J ("THE ORIGINAL LONG-RANGE ATTRACTANT" pinned top bar) + Whitetail Institute ("Industry Founders" since 1988 framing).
**Adopt:** A single always-visible top-of-page strip with GB's most defensible claim. **Recommendation:** "**KANSAS-BORN. FIELD-TESTED. HUNTER-OWNED. SINCE 2017.**" — no promo, no discount, no announcement. Just the heritage claim, persistent on every page.
**Morph for GB Feeds:** Set in the display face, reverse-out white on near-black, with an **inline mile-marker icon** (`KS-7,500`) referencing the antler-inches stat. The stat number itself updates dynamically (see §6).

## 3. Clichés to AVOID for this industry

Concrete and tactical. Direct comps have already burned every default; here is the explicit don't-list.

1. **Orange-and-camo or red-and-black "hunter" palettes.** Big & J (yellow-gold + blue), Boss Buck (crimson + charcoal), Antler King (orange + 6 mismatched category pills), BRCC's predecessors. This palette family is the visual language of every $5.99-bag Tractor Supply private-label brand. **Reject categorically.**
2. **Dead-buck-on-tailgate / trophy grip-and-grin photography.** Used implicitly across Whitetail's category pages, Big & J's lifestyle assets, and every social-media submission deer-feed brand re-shares. Pivot to working/process imagery (Greg planting, feeders staked at golden hour, trail-cam stills, deer-in-habitat).
3. **Wide horizontal Slider Revolution / Splide hero carousel.** Big & J, Whitetail, Boss Buck. Signals "2018 Shopify". Reject. Static cinematic single-frame hero or one orchestrated scroll moment instead.
4. **Inter / Roboto / Open Sans / Montserrat / Lato / Helvetica Neue body defaults.** Antler King (Inter only — total Shopify-Horizon default), Big & J (Montserrat), Record Rack (Work Sans — Cargill default), GB today (Montserrat). Every one signals "I downloaded the theme." Pick something the deer-feed category has never shown.
5. **Stock Shopify / WordPress / GoDaddy hero with announcement bar + 4-up product carousel + "About Us" box.** Boss Buck, Big & J, Antler King, Record Rack, GB today. **Reject the entire layout grammar.** The home page must not look like a templated DTC.
6. **"We are passionate about deer hunting" / "Hunter-founded, hunter-driven" boilerplate.** Every direct comp's About page. GB has Greg's actual 2017 founding narrative — use his words, not boilerplate.
7. **Centered logo + 6-link horizontal nav + cart icon** (BigCommerce/Shopify default). Whitetail, Big & J, Antler King, Boss Buck. Reject. Asymmetric or stamp-style header instead.
8. **Trust-badge clutter** (PCI / TrustedSite / SSL / "Free shipping over $X" stack above footer). GB today carries a TrustedSite badge with the wrong site ID. Strip the badge row entirely; replace with one earned proof (the antler-inches counter — see §6).
9. **Colored category-pill secondary nav with 6 mismatched accent hues** (Antler King's signature "feature" — and its biggest aesthetic failure). Reject.
10. **Stock product-on-white seamless** as the sole product photography mode. Boss Buck does this on category pages despite environmental hero shots elsewhere — the inconsistency cheapens the brand. Either commit to environment everywhere, or contrast clinical-on-white intentionally (Sitka model).
11. **Generic "Shop Now" / "Learn More" / "Discover" CTA copy.** Big & J, Boss Buck. Replace with imperatives in Greg's voice ("Drop a Bag", "Pull a Pallet", "Build Your Program").
12. **Light-mode-only with no accent atmosphere** (Record Rack — abundant whitespace becomes corporate-hospital). The bone canvas needs grain, paper texture, or stamped edges.
13. **AI-generated wildlife illustration / clean vector deer silhouettes.** Antler King's deer-skull mark is fine; nobody else's is. Reject any new vector deer art entirely. Use scanned scratchboard, woodcut, or photographic-only.

## 4. The aesthetic direction call

**Direction: "Kansas Field Logbook."**

> The site reads like a small-batch Kansas farmer's stand-by-stand hunting logbook that happens to ship deer feed. The dominant surface is **warm bone paper** (`#EDE7D9`, the color of a 1980s ag-extension service almanac) with **deep loam-black ink** (`#0F0E0B`) printed on it; everything sits inside a narrow inch-wide left margin marked with date / county / wind / weight stamps in monospace. The display face is **Tusker Grotesk** (a wide, slab-built industrial display family — narrower than Druk, more masculine than YETI Open, never used in deer-feed) set in heavy weight uppercase for headlines and bag-tag stat triptychs; body is **GT Sectra Display** (a modern angular serif with newspaper bones — refined, slightly aristocratic, completely against the category default) for the founder narrative, journal entries, and testimonials. Background atmosphere is layered: a subtle paper-fiber noise texture, faint horizontal hairlines every 24px (like ruled logbook paper), and on hero plates a low-opacity scanned-grain overlay that gives every photo the 35mm-on-newsprint feel. The accent is **a single saturated color: blaze brick `#B33A1A`** — not orange, not red, the color of dried oxblood paint on a barn — used exclusively for the "ANTLER INCHES HARVESTED" live counter, the Field Club member badges, and a single 1px underline on hovered links. The signature scroll moment is **one** — a 30-second-long single-pin scroll-tied sequence on the home page where the giant antler-inches counter (set in 240px Tusker) ticks from "0" up to the live total over the course of the user's scroll, while a real Kansas trail-cam plate cross-fades behind it from a black-and-white scan to a full-color wildlife frame. After that one moment, the rest of the site is motion-quiet: bag-tag stat reveals on PDP, sticky bottom add-to-cart on mobile, no parallax anywhere else. Implementation in Next.js 15 + Framer Motion + GSAP scroll-pin + Lenis smooth-scroll gated to the one home moment — minimal everywhere else. Every decision says: this is Greg's logbook, you are reading his actual notes, and the bag of feed is the receipt of his work.

## 5. Color / type / motion principles

**Color.** Reject the orange-and-camo cliché entirely; reject hunter-red; reject Whitetail's topaz; reject BRCC's tan-gold. Anchor on a **bone-paper neutral + loam-ink near-black + ONE oxblood-brick accent (`#B33A1A`)**, with a 6-step warm-gray ladder as connective tissue. The palette family is *agricultural almanac*, not *hunting catalog*. Every photo, every section, every CTA reads against the same warm bone canvas — the brand recognition is the warmth itself. No dark-mode toggle. No cool grays. No `#000` pure black anywhere — always loam-ink `#0F0E0B`.

**Type.** Display is **Tusker Grotesk** (Lost Type Co-op), wide industrial slab-grotesk used uppercase for H1/H2/buttons/bag-tag stats — never seen in deer-feed; reads as Kansas grain-elevator signage rather than tactical military. Body is **GT Sectra Display** (Grilli Type), an angular contemporary serif with newspaper bones for journal entries / founder narrative / testimonials — the serif is the move that breaks the entire deer-feed category (every comp is sans-only). The pairing — wide industrial display + refined newspaper serif — signals *almanac, not catalog*. The pair fits because GB's voice is hunter-to-hunter blue-collar (Tusker carries this) but the editorial frame is Greg's first-person logbook (Sectra carries this). Tertiary: monospace **JetBrains Mono** for the date/county/wind stamps, lot numbers, antler-inch counter ticks. Three faces total — none of them Inter, Montserrat, Roboto, Open Sans, or any system-stack default.

**Motion.** ONE signature moment: the home-page **scroll-pinned antler-inches counter ticker** (Lenis-smooth, GSAP-pinned, ~30 seconds of scroll, one full viewport, the counter climbs from 0 to the live aggregate while the trail-cam plate cross-fades from grayscale scan to full color behind it). After that, motion is restraint-mode: 200ms color-fade button hovers, 600ms fade-up section reveals at 25% viewport, sticky mobile add-to-cart, hover-scale on PDP imagery (subtle, ≤1.02). Guardrail: **no parallax anywhere except the one moment, no scroll-jacking outside the one moment, full `prefers-reduced-motion` respect that replaces the moment with a static counter + still photo.** Maximalism in the one moment; precision restraint everywhere else.

## 6. The signature move

**The Live Antler Inches Counter + Kansas-County Pin Map** — a single composite home-page module nobody in deer-feed has.

A real-time counter at the top of the home page reads **"ANTLER INCHES HARVESTED WITH GB FEEDS"** in 240px Tusker Grotesk, ticking up live as Greg logs new harvests through a small admin form. Each tick triggers a faint chime and a numerical roll. Below the counter is a small **interactive Kansas map** rendered as topographic county outlines (USGS-style) where every reported harvest drops a tiny brick-red pin; hovering a pin reveals the customer first-name + date + score (e.g. "Dylan, 11/14/2024, 142" 8-pt"). The map zooms only to Kansas (the brand is Kansas-tested — this is the proof). The counter and map together resolve the existing brand-stat inconsistency (5,000 vs 7,500 vs 10,000 inches across three pages) by replacing all three with a single live source of truth that grows in real time. Buildable as a Next.js static-export ISR-style page that revalidates on a JSON file pushed via a one-line admin form; no DB, no auth, no backend — Greg pushes a PR or hits a Cloudflare Worker form, the JSON updates, the page rebuilds, the counter ticks. The single signature scroll-pinned moment described in §5 is what *animates* this counter on first load.

## Phase 3D Architecture Feasibility Notes — react-architect

> Stack: Next.js 15 + React 19 + TypeScript + Tailwind v4 + Framer Motion + GSAP + Lenis. `output: 'export'`. Cloudflare Pages via Wrangler.
> Author: react-architect. Date: 2026-05-06.

### Principle-by-Principle Feasibility Grade

---

#### 1. 24px Ruled Hairlines + Paper-Fiber Noise + Scanned-Grain Overlay on Hero Plates

| Axis | Rating |
|---|---|
| Cost | **cheap** |
| Static-export compatibility | **compatible** |
| Component pattern | Pure CSS/Tailwind — no React component needed beyond a `<BackgroundTexture>` wrapper div |

**Detail.** The 24px ruled hairlines are a repeating CSS `background-image: repeating-linear-gradient(...)` applied at the `<body>` or a persistent layout wrapper — one Tailwind arbitrary utility or a single CSS custom property. The paper-fiber noise is a small (≤ 8 KB) SVG `<feTurbulence>` filter or a WebP grain tile baked at build time, mounted as a CSS `background-image` on the hero section with `mix-blend-mode: multiply` and `opacity: 0.06–0.10`. The scanned-grain overlay on hero plates is the same technique — a fixed-position pseudo-element with `pointer-events: none`. Zero JS. Zero runtime cost. Total implementation: one `globals.css` block + one `HeroGrain.tsx` wrapper client component (needed only to conditionally suppress for `prefers-reduced-motion`, though grain at that opacity is motion-neutral and typically exempt from the reduced-motion contract). Pre-generate the grain tile as a 256×256 WebP at build prep time; reference via `public/textures/grain.webp`.

---

#### 2. Bone/Loam Color System + Oxblood Single-Accent + 6-Step Warm-Gray Ladder. Dark Mode Recommendation.

| Axis | Rating |
|---|---|
| Cost | **cheap** |
| Static-export compatibility | **compatible** |
| Component pattern | Tailwind v4 CSS custom-property token system (`@theme` block in `globals.css`); no JS color-switch logic |

**Detail.** Define the full palette as CSS custom properties inside Tailwind v4's `@theme {}` block. The 6-step warm-gray ladder (`--color-stone-1` through `--color-stone-6`) maps to Tailwind semantic tokens. The oxblood `#B33A1A` is a single token (`--color-accent`) used only where the synthesis permits — no secondary accent tokens. The bone `#EDE7D9` and loam `#0F0E0B` are `--color-surface` and `--color-ink`. This is a ≤ 30-line CSS block.

**Dark mode recommendation: do not implement.** The bone-paper aesthetic is a physical object simulation. A dark-mode inversion (bone → near-black, loam → bone) destroys the logbook metaphor entirely — the brand IS the warm surface. Every competitor that has tried a dark mode on a warm-paper brand has ended up with a muddy charcoal that reads neither as dark-mode-native nor as the brand. More practically: the synthesis explicitly calls for "No dark-mode toggle. No cool grays." — this is correct. Implementing `prefers-color-scheme: dark` as a pure CSS media query (without a toggle) would mean users in dark OS mode see an inverted experience with zero brand control. **Recommendation: lock to light mode only.** Apply `<html style="color-scheme: light only">` in `layout.tsx` — one line that tells the browser not to attempt any inversion. No `dark:` Tailwind variants anywhere in the codebase.

#### 3. Tusker Grotesk + GT Sectra Display + JetBrains Mono. Free Substitutes.

| Axis | Rating |
|---|---|
| Cost (commercial licensing) | **moderate to expensive** — Tusker Grotesk (Lost Type "pay what you want" for personal, commercial license required for commercial use); GT Sectra Display (Grilli Type, commercial license ~$200–$600/style depending on pageview tier) |
| Cost (substitution path) | **cheap** — Google Fonts + `next/font` FOUT-free loading |
| Static-export compatibility | **compatible** (Google Fonts via `next/font/google` inlines CSS at build; zero runtime network dependency) |
| Component pattern | `next/font/google` in `layout.tsx` → CSS variables → Tailwind `fontFamily` tokens |

**Commercial font verdict.** Tusker Grotesk and GT Sectra Display cannot be self-hosted or loaded via `next/font` without valid commercial licenses. If budget permits a type spend (~$300–$800 total), both are buildable under license with manual self-hosting via `next/font/local`. If budget does not permit, the substitution trio below preserves the full aesthetic intent.

**Recommended free substitution trio:**

| Role | Commercial target | Free substitute | Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|
| Wide industrial display (H1/H2/buttons/stat triptychs) | Tusker Grotesk | **[Bebas Neue](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Bebas+Neue)** | All-caps condensed sans, wide optically when tracked (letter-spacing: 0.08em), Kansas grain-elevator register — zero deer-feed precedent at this weight. Note: Bebas is ALL CAPS only, which matches the synthesis's uppercase-only H1/H2 mandate. Zero variants needed. |
| Angular newspaper serif (body / journal / testimonials) | GT Sectra Display | **[DM Serif Display](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/DM+Serif+Display)** | High-contrast didone structure with sharp angular serifs; newspaper bones, editorial authority; completely absent from the deer-feed competitive set; pairs naturally against a compressed sans. Italic variant carries the "Greg's voice" editorial entries. |
| Monospace stamps (date/county/wind/lot numbers/counter) | JetBrains Mono | **[JetBrains Mono](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/JetBrains+Mono)** | JetBrains Mono IS available free on Google Fonts under OFL license — no substitution needed. Load via `next/font/google`. |

**FOUT mitigation.** With `next/font/google`, fonts are inlined into the CSS bundle at build time and served from Cloudflare Pages' own edge — no FOUT on first paint. Add `display: 'swap'` and `preload: true` on the display face. The synthesis's bone-paper background means a brief flash of unstyled large type is less visually jarring than on a pure-white canvas, but the `next/font` path eliminates it regardless.

#### 4. Signature Scroll-Pinned Home Moment — Antler-Inches Counter + Kansas County Map + Grayscale-to-Color Crossfade

| Axis | Rating |
|---|---|
| Cost | **expensive** (4–6 dev days) — the most complex single component in the site; warrants the investment given it is the ONE signature moment |
| Static-export compatibility | **compatible-with-tradeoff** — see data-source proposal below |
| Component pattern | `'use client'` component with `dynamic(() => import('./ScrollPinSequence'), { ssr: false })` — GSAP `ScrollTrigger.pin()` + `gsap.to()` counter tween + CSS `filter: grayscale()` crossfade + inline SVG Kansas county map |

**Static-export compatibility and data source.**
The "live" antler-inches counter needs a source of truth that survives `output: 'export'`. Three options ranked:

1. **Recommended — Cloudflare Worker + build-time JSON bake.** Greg submits harvests via a minimal Cloudflare Worker form endpoint (POST → updates a KV entry). A GitHub Actions deploy trigger (or manual `wrangler pages deploy`) re-runs `next build` which fetches the KV value at build time and bakes the counter's target number into `public/data/harvests.json`. The component reads the JSON at runtime; the counter always shows the value as of last deploy. No database, no auth surface, no SSR needed. A weekly or per-harvest deploy cadence keeps the number current. This is the pattern the synthesis already describes ("Greg pushes a PR or hits a Cloudflare Worker form, the JSON updates, the page rebuilds").

2. **Alternative — Static JSON in repo, Greg PRs updates.** Simpler for Phase 4 MVP. Greg (or whoever manages content) edits `public/data/harvests.json` directly and pushes to `main`. GitHub Actions rebuilds and deploys. Viable for the current scale (a handful of harvests per season). Add to the `README`: "To log a new harvest: edit `public/data/harvests.json`, push to main."

3. **Do not use — ISR / `revalidate`.** `output: 'export'` disables ISR entirely. Any `revalidate` directive in route handlers is silently ignored at export time. This is a known Next.js 15 constraint.

**Performance budget.** The primary risks on this component are:

- **Kansas SVG county map.** Use the USGS/Census Bureau Kansas county SVG (public domain, available at census.gov/geo). Clean version is ~80 KB raw; simplify with mapshaper.org to ≤ 20 KB before committing. Inline as a React SVG component (not an `<img>`) so individual county `<path>` elements are addressable for the brick-red pin drop interaction. No third-party mapping library (Leaflet, Mapbox) needed — this is a static SVG with hover tooltips, not a tile map.
- **GSAP ScrollTrigger + pin.** Import GSAP + ScrollTrigger only in this one `'use client'` component via dynamic import with `ssr: false`. This isolates the GSAP payload (~60 KB min+gz for core + ScrollTrigger) to the home route only. Other routes pay zero GSAP cost if they do not import it. Lenis is already in the stack; GSAP ScrollTrigger must call `ScrollTrigger.scrollerProxy()` to sync with Lenis — this is a required ~5-line setup that every Lenis+GSAP integration needs; document it in the component's JSDoc.
- **Grayscale-to-color crossfade.** This is a CSS `filter: grayscale(1→0)` tween on the hero `<img>` element, driven by the GSAP timeline's progress. No canvas compositing, no WebGL — pure CSS filter on a single `<img>`. The two states (grayscale, full-color) are the same image file; no double download.
- **Total JS payload for the home route.** Estimate: React 19 core + Next.js runtime (~45 KB gz) + Framer Motion tree-shaken to used components (~12 KB gz) + GSAP + ScrollTrigger dynamically loaded (~60 KB gz, home only) + Lenis (~8 KB gz) = ~125 KB gz on home. Acceptable for a cinematic hero moment; within Core Web Vitals LCP budget if the hero image is properly sized and `priority` preloaded.

**`prefers-reduced-motion` fallback.** The component checks `window.matchMedia('(prefers-reduced-motion: reduce)')` on mount (via `useEffect`). When true: the scroll pin is not initialized; the counter displays the baked-in final number immediately (no ticking animation); the trail-cam plate renders in full color immediately (no grayscale → color transition); the Kansas map renders with all pins visible at once (no sequential drop animation). The full logbook layout is still present — the user still sees the counter, the map, the trail-cam image — they just see the final state without motion. This is a meaningful, non-degraded experience.

### Top 5 Architecture Risks with Mitigations

- **Bundle bloat from GSAP + Framer + Lenis loaded globally.** Mitigation: dynamic-import GSAP + ScrollTrigger only into the scroll-pin home component (`ssr: false`); load Lenis only in `SmoothScrollProvider` which wraps only routes that need it; tree-shake Framer Motion to used exports only.

- **iOS Safari momentum-scroll conflict with GSAP scroll-pin.** Mitigation: GSAP ScrollTrigger's `normalizeScroll: true` + Lenis's `touchMultiplier` tuning; test on Safari/iOS 17+ before any other browser; the scroll-pin is one isolated component, so a Safari-specific bail-out path (detect `CSS.supports('-webkit-touch-callout','none')`) can disable the pin and fall back to the reduced-motion static state.

- **FOUT on Bebas Neue display face at 240px on first paint.** Mitigation: `next/font/google` with `preload: true` and `display: 'swap'` inlines the font CSS into the initial HTML shell; the 240px counter is inside the scroll-pin section (not above the fold on load), so it is not in the LCP critical path and FOUT there is imperceptible.

- **Cloudflare Worker live-counter data source drift if Greg never redeploys.** Mitigation: document the rebuild trigger clearly; additionally render the counter's baked value with a small "as of [deploy date]" monospace stamp so the number's age is transparent to users — turns a potential trust problem into a logbook authenticity signal.

- **Kansas SVG county map accessibility and mobile hit targets.** Mitigation: each county `<path>` gets an `aria-label` with county name; pin hover tooltips are also keyboard-focusable (`tabIndex={0}` + `onFocus`); on mobile viewports below 480px, collapse the map to a static list of county names with harvest counts rather than an interactive SVG — simpler and more legible on a small screen.
